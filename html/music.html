Cristofori's Dream was my first recorded piece after dedicating more time and energy to the piano. The legato runs and dramatic build made it a fun first piece.

Recorded on May 5, 2013 at the age of 11.
Waterfall is one of the more memorable pieces that I have recorded. This was for a competition among the students of my piano instructor called "The Most Bodacious Musician" which I won with this piece.

Recorded on May 17, 2014 at the age of 12.
Memphis Stomp, a quick, bouncy piece by Dave Grusin was one of my favorites to play. The way the notes bounced the keyboard was statisfying and addicting. This was definitely one of the "poundy" pieces that got old for Mom quickly. I also played this for my 8th grade promotion although I admittedly played it far too fast.

Recorded on June 28, 2015 at the age of 13.
Star Wars was one of the more difficult pieces that I prepared from a technical standpoint. Quick runs and tricky rhythms made this cover tricky to master. Around this point I began to switch my interest from loud, popular pieces to more emotional, dynamically challenging arrangements.

Recorded on February 26, 2016 at the age of 14.
Turnabout, a shorter piece out of a theory book (if I remember correctly) launched me into an addiction with classical music. It was the emotion of the pieces that really drew me in. So really, Turnabout became a pivot moment for me.

Recorded on June 13, 2017 at the age of 15.
I have a love for this piece. The rhythm in the left hand counterposes that of the right hand with 16th notes against triplets which creates a beautiful wave of sound. Dynamically, Chopin's Fantasie Impromptu presents an interesting challenge as well. My forever battle with classical music is playing too fast, and as you might notice in this recording, I am desperately trying to play much slower.

Recorded on June 7, 2019 at the age of 17.
If I had to pick one piece to play for the rest of my life, it would be Chopin's Ballade No. 1 in G Minor. This piece combines beautiful themes with dramatic builds and an incredible harmonic "explosion" at the end. I spent the majority of my senior year trying to perfect this piece. While being my favorite, the Ballade was also the most frustrating. Recording was incredibly difficult, as the piece is just over 10 minutes long. It's a wonderful piece though.

Recorded on July 30, 2020 at the age of 18.
